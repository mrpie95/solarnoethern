Viewing factors beyond Kp
Cloud cover — most common reason people miss aurora. Check forecast for the northern horizon direction.
Light pollution — urban glow drowns out faint aurora. From NL: Lauwersoog, Frisian islands, Veluwe, Hoge Rielen in BE.
Moon phase — full moon washes out faint displays. New moon ± 5 days is ideal.
Time of night — substorms peak 22:00–02:00 local time.
Equinox bonus — March and September have ~2× more storms (Russell–McPherron effect).
Solar cycle — we're near Solar Cycle 25 maximum. 2024–2026 are the best years in two decades.
Caveats of this model
The threshold formula min Kp = (66 − mag lat) / 2 gives naked-eye visibility. Long-exposure photography captures aurora 1–2 Kp below this.
The real auroral oval is squashed toward the night side. This circular model approximates the dayside extent.
Extreme storms (G5) produce red SAR arcs visible much further south than either model shows — May 2024 aurora reached Mexico.
OVATION is itself a model, not direct observation. It infers aurora from particle precipitation measured at L1 (~1.5M km upstream).
